### 1. Project Objectives & Goals
**Overall Objective**  
Develop a web application designed to help users discover healthy, affordable food options from local stores. The application will leverage large language models (LLMs) to provide detailed nutritional information on products which allow users to make more informed choices when it comes to their diet. Users can create and manage personal grocery list(s), find and share recipes with the community, and even use an AI-powered chatbot to generate grocery lists automatically. Finally, Foodable offers users a comprehensive, AI-enhanced platform for users to make healthier and smarter food choices, with a strong social component with other food enthusiasts.

**Goals**  
Application must contain the following:  
1. Easily discover nutritious and budget-friendly products in their local area.
2. Access real-time nutritional information powered by AI for informed decision-making.
3. Create, customize, and manage grocery lists that are aligned with their health goals and shopping preferences.
4. Share recipes and grocery lists within a community, fostering a collaborative and educational environment around food and nutrition.
5. Integrate AI to automate routine tasks, like generating grocery lists, reducing the time spent on meal planning and shopping.
6. Build a social network where users can engage with others through public profiles, sharing, following, and interacting with like-minded individuals.

### 8. Conflict Management (what will you do if there is disagreement on how to proceed?).  
When a conflict arises (like a disagreement), the first thing to do is listen to both sides. All parties involved in the conflict should outline their point of view and respectfully provide feedback on what they disagree with. Once all sides have given their piece, then a compromise can be reached. Ideally, this will be done through a meeting (either on Teams or Zoom). Any team member who believes that their schedule may impact their ability to deliver progress or communicate effectively should notify the team as soon as possible. 

### 9. Communication Expectations
All team members are expected to have easy access to Teams and have notifications enabled for important information and announcements. Team members who are mentioned in conversation have an expectation to respond within a timely manner. Due to the nature of timezones, obligations, and anything that can interfere with responding, there should be some grace given. 
**...within 24 hours...**
If the notified team member has not responded within 24 hours of being notified (especially if it is something crucial), then the team can mention them again.
**...within 48 hours...**
If there is no response within 48 hours, the team may email the directly (as maybe they have notifications off). The notified team member should by this point have responded, or at least given a reason for their lack of communication. 


### 10. Personal Expectations
Our team is comprised of different developers with varied skills and skillsets. Those team members should embark on a project or feature if they can reasonably complete the task. In other words, they should understand their limits. For some cases, that team member can learn as they go, but only if they believe that they have enough time to do both learning how to do complete their project while also developing it at the same time. If at any point a team member feels as if they will be unable to complete the task, **notify the team immediantly**. Task completion is more important than ego.
